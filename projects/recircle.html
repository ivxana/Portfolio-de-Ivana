03 Sustainability · AI · Circular Economy

ReCircle

The End of Waste.

Overview

ReCircle is a closed-loop home resource system designed to automatically identify, separate, and convert household waste into usable outputs - transforming homes from waste producers into distributed resource generators.

Instead of improving traditional recycling systems, ReCircle explored an entirely different model: what if waste never became waste in the first place?

TYPE Systems Concept
FOCUS Sustainability · AI
MODEL Circular Economy

Why We Built It

The home becomes a mini resource plant.

Modern waste systems are fundamentally reactive. Current recycling infrastructure depends on manual sorting, long transportation chains, centralized processing, and contamination-prone systems. Even recyclable materials frequently end up landfilled, burned, exported, or polluted into ecosystems.

ReCircle was built around a simple shift in framing: instead of asking how to recycle better, we asked what it would look like to stop producing waste altogether.

System Workflow

From mixed input to usable output.

ReCircle uses AI sensing and automated material routing to process mixed household waste locally - no manual sorting required.

Why It Matters

Waste is not something to remove - it’s something to convert.

  • Waste transportation decreases
  • Contamination reduced at the source
  • Homes produce usable outputs locally
  • Fewer materials reach landfills and oceans
  • Value loops remain inside communities

Reflection

Solving environmental problems may require redesigning systems entirely - not simply optimizing the ones that already exist.

ReCircle became an exploration of how environmental infrastructure could evolve beyond recycling itself - sitting at the intersection of AI sensing, sustainability, automation, circular economy design, and decentralized infrastructure.

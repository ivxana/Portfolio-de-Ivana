02 Innovation · Emerging Tech · Consulting

TKS

Where I learned to build at speed.

Overview

TKS is a global innovation program for ambitious young people working on the world’s most important problems. Fellows work on real challenges in emerging tech, consulting, and product - with no hand-holding and no predetermined answers.

As an Innovate Fellow, I worked across consulting challenges, a hackathon, and self-directed builds spanning conflict prevention, misinformation, biosensing, and clean energy.

TKS is highly selective. I applied three days before the deadline and was not only accepted but offered a 75% scholarship.

The TKS Effect

I came in knowing how to study. I left knowing how to move.

Before TKS, I waited. For instructions. For permission. For someone to tell me I was ready. TKS didn’t give me any of that - and that was the point.

The program hands you hard problems with no brief and no ceiling. You figure out what to build, who to talk to, and how to make something real out of almost nothing. I cold-emailed researchers I had no business contacting. I walked into domains I knew nothing about and had to form opinions anyway. I defended those opinions in front of people who knew far more than I did.

What changed wasn’t my knowledge base. It was my relationship to not knowing. I stopped waiting to feel ready. I started treating “I don’t know yet” as a starting point.

That’s the skill. And it doesn’t come from a classroom.

Reflections

TKS isn’t about any one project.

It’s about who you become between projects. The moment you realize you can just… do things. No permission required. No brief handed to you. Just a problem, a deadline, and the choice to move anyway.

I joined not knowing what I was signing up for. I left with a different relationship to hard problems. Not fearless - but willing. And I think willing is actually more useful.

You don’t need permission to work on hard problems. You just need to know who to email.

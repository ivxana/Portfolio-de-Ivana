02 Innovation · Emerging Tech · Consulting

TKS

Where I learned to build at speed.

Overview

TKS is a global innovation program focused on emerging technologies, systems thinking, and solving real-world problems through hands-on building and research.

As an Innovate Fellow, I worked on consulting challenges, hackathons, and self-directed builds across civic tech, conflict prevention, and clean energy.

ROLE Innovate Fellow
FOCUS AI · Policy
STATUS Sept 2025 – June 2026

Why It Mattered

Most programs teach you to memorize. TKS taught me to ship.

I cold-emailed PhD researchers. I researched domains I knew nothing about. I defended ideas in front of judges spanning multiple domains. I figured out where to start when nobody handed me a brief.

That’s the skill. And it doesn’t come from a classroom.

Key Projects

Three builds. Three different kinds of hard.

01

UN South Sudan

Consulting

A 30-day consulting challenge to address pastoralist conflict in South Sudan. I led narrative, outreach, and communication for our 4-person team.

40+ cold emails to humanitarian & geospatial experts
8 expert meetings secured
#1 top submission in cohort
What it taught me
  • How to work within constraints - 30 days, no domain knowledge, a problem affecting real lives
  • Access to support changes outcomes - finding the right experts unlocked everything
  • How to build systems-thinking around problems that don’t have one right answer

02

UNSPUN

Misinformation

A framework that scores claims 0–100 and identifies specific manipulation tactics: emotional appeal, cherry picking, loaded language, missing context, false connection.

The hardest part wasn’t building the tool - it was that there’s no agreed-upon framework for what manipulation even is.

A huge part of this project was determining what manipulation actually is, and figuring out how to calculate it without injecting my own bias into the model. It stress-tested my own understanding and forced me to define things carefully.

It’s a proof of concept more than a product - but the underlying definitional work was the real win.

03

BioVerify

Hackathon

A low-cost biosensing prototype to detect influenza signals in school air. Built in 2 hours during a hackathon. Won.

A proof of how quickly a team can move from idea to working prototype when the constraints force focus. Sometimes the time limit is the feature.

04

ReCircle

Product

A closed-loop home resource system designed to eliminate waste by converting household materials into usable outputs in real-time - a fundamentally different model than recycling.

The hardest part wasn’t the technical architecture - it was reframing the problem. Recycling is still waste-management thinking. ReCircle starts from a different premise: what if the concept of waste didn’t exist?

What it taught me
  • How to challenge the problem definition itself, not just solve for it
  • Systems thinking at the scale of a home - not an institution or a government
  • Building in a domain I didn’t come from forces clarity - you can’t rely on jargon

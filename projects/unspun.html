02 NLP · AI · Misinformation

UNSPUN

Detecting Emotional Manipulation in Language

Overview

UNSPUN explores how emotionally manipulative language shapes perception online.

Built as an NLP-powered manipulation detection system, the project analyzes headlines, claims, and public-facing statements to identify rhetorical tactics commonly used to influence belief, amplify outrage, and manufacture credibility.

TYPE NLP Research Project
FOCUS AI · Misinformation
DEMO unspun.streamlit.app ↗︎
ARTICLE Read on Medium ↗︎

The Question

What if misinformation detection focused not only on factual accuracy — but on manipulation itself?

As AI-generated media, algorithmic feeds, and emotionally optimized content continue scaling, distinguishing trustworthy information becomes increasingly difficult.

UNSPUN was built to explore how manipulation patterns in language could be surfaced before they become normalized as truth.

The Core Insight

Manipulation is often structural.

Emotionally manipulative content frequently relies on recurring rhetorical patterns. These signals appear across political messaging, headlines, social media, viral misinformation, and AI-generated content.

MANIPULATION PATTERNS

  • Urgency framing
  • Fear amplification
  • Authority appeals
  • False certainty
  • Outrage optimization
  • Loaded language

SURFACES ANALYZED

  • Political messaging
  • Headlines
  • Social media
  • Viral misinformation
  • AI-generated content

What It Does

Manipulation Detection Interface

Users can paste claims, headlines, social posts, articles, or public statements. The system analyzes the language and highlights potential manipulation indicators.

Emotional Trigger
Fear Amplification
Authority Appeal
Urgency Framing
Loaded Language
False Certainty

Rather than deciding what is “true” or “false,” the system helps users better understand how language may be shaping perception.

Technical Exploration

Pattern detection ≠ truth detection.

One of the central realizations from the project: emotionally charged language can still be completely accurate. Calm language can still be deeply misleading. That distinction became foundational to how the system was framed.

EXPLORED

  • NLP classification systems
  • Transformer-based language models
  • Misinformation datasets
  • Explainability challenges
  • Real-time language analysis
  • Bias and trust in AI systems

Why It Matters

As AI-generated information scales, understanding how language influences perception becomes more important than ever.

UNSPUN explored how explainable AI systems could help people think more critically, recognize emotional framing, understand manipulation tactics, and engage with information more intentionally.

My Role

  • NLP & misinformation systems research
  • Manipulation framework exploration
  • Interface and UX direction
  • Explainability & trust framing
  • Translating technical concepts into user-facing systems

Reflection

If AI systems increasingly shape what people see, trust, and believe — how do we ensure people still understand the mechanisms influencing them?

UNSPUN ultimately became less about misinformation classification and more about the relationship between language, trust, power, and perception.

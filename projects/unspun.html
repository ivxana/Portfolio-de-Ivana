UNSPUN

Detecting Emotional Manipulation in Language

Overview

UNSPUN is an NLP-powered system that analyzes headlines, claims, and public-facing statements to identify rhetorical tactics used to influence belief, amplify outrage, and manufacture credibility online.

Rather than focusing solely on factual accuracy, the project explores how emotionally optimized language shapes perception across digital media.

TYPE NLP Research Project
FOCUS AI · Information Integrity · Media Literacy
DEMO unspun.streamlit.app ↗︎
ARTICLE Read on Medium ↗︎

The Question

What happens when misinformation becomes emotionally persuasive before it becomes factually convincing?

As AI-generated media and algorithmic feeds scale, distinguishing trustworthy information becomes increasingly difficult. UNSPUN was built to explore whether manipulation patterns in language could be surfaced before they become normalized as truth.

The Core Insight

Manipulation is often structural.

Across political messaging, viral headlines, social posts, and AI-generated content, emotionally manipulative communication tends to rely on recurring rhetorical patterns.

PATTERNS EXPLORED

  • Urgency framing
  • Fear amplification
  • Authority appeals
  • False certainty
  • Outrage optimization
  • Loaded language

SURFACES ANALYZED

  • Political messaging
  • Headlines
  • Social media
  • Viral misinformation
  • AI-generated content

What It Does

Manipulation Detection Interface

Users can paste headlines, claims, articles, or public statements into the interface. The system analyzes the text and highlights potential manipulation indicators such as:

Emotional Triggers
Fear Amplification
Authority Appeals
Loaded Language
False Certainty
Urgency Framing

Rather than labeling content as “true” or “false,” UNSPUN helps users better understand how language may be influencing interpretation.

Technical Exploration

Pattern detection ≠ truth detection.

One of the central realizations from the project:

Emotionally charged language can still be accurate, while calm language can still be deeply misleading.

That distinction became foundational to how the system was framed.

EXPLORED

  • NLP classification systems
  • Transformer-based language models
  • Misinformation datasets
  • Explainability challenges
  • Real-time language analysis
  • Bias and trust in AI systems

Why It Matters

As AI-generated information becomes increasingly scalable, understanding how language shapes interpretation becomes more important than ever.

UNSPUN explored how explainable AI systems could encourage more intentional engagement with information by helping users recognize emotional framing, rhetorical pressure, and manipulation tactics in real time.

My Role

  • NLP & misinformation systems research
  • Manipulation framework exploration
  • Interface and UX direction
  • Explainability & trust framing
  • Translating technical systems into user-facing experiences

Reflection

UNSPUN ultimately became less about misinformation classification and more about the relationship between language, trust, power, and perception.

The project explored a broader question:

If AI increasingly shapes what people see and believe, how do we ensure people still understand the mechanisms influencing them?

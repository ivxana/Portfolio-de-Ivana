Why This Matters

Not everything I do is career-adjacent. These aren’t.

DECA and Mock Trial aren’t on this portfolio because they make me look well-rounded. They’re here because I genuinely love them - the pressure, the preparation, the feeling of walking into a room and having to think fast. These are things I do because I want to, not because they’re strategic.

That said, they’re also two very different things - and the difference matters.

Competitions

Two arenas. Two completely different experiences.

DECA

Business Competition

Principles of Business Management & Administration (PBM) · Provincial Competitor

DECA is fundamentally individual. You walk into a room alone, read a case you’ve never seen before, have ten minutes to structure a response, and then defend it in front of judges who are actively looking for holes. There’s no team to fall back on. It’s just you, a sheet of paper, and whatever you can put together under pressure.

I was new to Canada. My chapter was small - 35 people. I had zero support. I taught myself everything by attending every DECA Office Hour I could find, and placed at both Regionals and Provincials anyway.

Top 10 PBM - Regionals
Top 10 Cluster Exam - Regionals
Top 20 Cluster Exam - Provincials, Feb 2026
Top 20 2nd Role Play - Provincials, Feb 2026

Mock Trial

Argumentation

Lawyer - Cross Examination

Mock Trial is the opposite of DECA - it’s entirely a team sport. You win together or you lose together, and the courtroom dynamic makes that real. You can crush your cross examination and still walk away with a loss if the rest of the case doesn’t hold. The pressure is different because it’s shared.

I almost didn’t join. My law teacher convinced me over weeks, making every accommodation to get me to say yes. And I almost quit twice - terrified of juggling too many things and doing none of them well.

But someone believed in me before I believed in myself. So I said yes.

That feeling when I’d walk away from the stand after crushing my cross examination - and turn around to a team ready to high five me - is one I hadn’t felt in a long time.

4th Final placement - missed the podium by a small margin
What Mock Trial taught me
  • How to find flaws in witnesses’ stories in real time - cross examination as a live skill
  • How to care about the team’s performance, not just my own
  • Sometimes there’s more joy in watching others grow than in winning yourself

Reflection

These aren’t career moves. They’re just things I love - the kind of hard that feels good, the kind of pressure I actually want to be under.

Business strategy and legal argumentation excite me in a way that has nothing to do with what they might signal on a resume. I compete because I like competing. That’s the whole reason.

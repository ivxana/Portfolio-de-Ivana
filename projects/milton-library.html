Milton Public Library

Youth Advisory Member  ·  Sept 2025 - Present

Overview

Giving back to the spaces that shaped me.

I grew up in libraries. Not just as a place to read - as a community. Libraries were where I felt safe, intellectually alive, and welcome when a lot of other spaces didn’t feel that way. They were a formative part of how I came to love learning and ideas.

Now I serve them. As a Youth Advisory Member at the Milton Public Library, I contribute to programming, digital literacy, and strategic direction.

What I Do

  • Design and run children’s programming that builds early literacy and curiosity
  • Teach coding and digital literacy workshops for young learners
  • Provide strategic input on book acquisitions - advising on titles that reflect the community
  • Represent the youth perspective in library planning and direction

Why Libraries

Libraries are one of the last truly public, truly open spaces. Free to enter, free to stay, free to learn. That’s not nothing. That’s everything.

The work I do here isn’t glamorous - it’s storytime planning, book lists, coding sessions for kids who may or may not care. But it’s the kind of quiet, consistent work that actually builds communities. Libraries showed up for me. I show up for them.

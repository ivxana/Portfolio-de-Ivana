Member, Vote16 Working Group  ·  May 2026 - Present

Overview

Expanding who gets to be a first-time voter.

The Canadian Council of Young Feminists’ Vote16 Working Group is working to extend voting rights to 16-17 year olds in Canada through Bill S-222. The work is about more than the vote itself - it’s about recognizing that young people are already active participants in civic life and should have formal say in decisions that affect them.

This connects directly to Tallied. Tallied makes voting information accessible to first-time voters; CCYF Vote16 is expanding who gets to be one.

The Connection to Tallied

Tallied lowers the barrier to understanding democracy. Vote16 lowers the barrier to participating in it. They’re the same project, two different angles.

What I Do

  • Senator outreach - making the case directly to legislators
  • Youth petition coordination and community mobilization
  • Op-eds and public writing in support of Vote16
  • Contributing youth perspective to the broader policy argument

Why It Matters

Sixteen-year-olds pay taxes, hold jobs, and are directly affected by government policy. The argument against letting them vote is almost always about perceived maturity - but maturity isn’t evenly distributed at any age, and it’s not the right bar anyway.

Civic participation is a habit. The earlier it forms, the more durable it becomes. Vote16 is an investment in the long-term health of Canadian democracy - and it’s one I’m proud to work toward.

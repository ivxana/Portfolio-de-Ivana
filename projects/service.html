03 Community · Mental Health · Youth Leadership

Service.

Small actions. Real impact.

Overview

I don’t just serve communities - I serve communities I’ve been part of. That’s the through-line.

Three years of consistent community work: running mental health programming for 2,000+ students at my school, supporting global STEM education for young women, and contributing to civic engagement nonprofits.

FOCUS Community · Mental Health
SPAN 3 Years
STATUS Ongoing

Why It Mattered

Building tools is one form of impact. Showing up for people, week after week, is another.

I do both because they teach different things. Tech teaches me to build systems. Service teaches me to stay when things are slow, unglamorous, and still necessary.

Roles

Four communities. One thread.

01

Knights for Safe Spaces

Sept 2025 – Present

Co-Executive

I joined this club as a new student - and as an international student, I saw the lack of mental health resources at my school firsthand, especially for people like me. I didn’t wait to be invited. I joined as an executive and won the role.

Now I’m building the resources I wish had existed when I arrived.
2,000+ students reached through mental health programming
100+ international students in targeted programming
30 volunteers led
Leadership learning
  • Asking “what am I missing?” before finalizing decisions
  • Designing for the people in the room who haven’t spoken yet
02

The Pink Stairs

April 2026 – Present

Outreach Executive

The natural evolution: I spent two years doing outreach for myself through TKS - cold-emailing researchers, building my own network. Now I’m doing outreach for a global nonprofit. Same skill, much bigger mission.

8.4M girls reached across 24 countries in STEM
24 countries
What I do
  • Build partnerships with mission-aligned organizations
  • Said no to leading the coding program (not qualified to teach fundamentals) - protected program quality
03

Canadian Council of Young Feminists

May 2026 – Present

Member, Vote16 Working Group

A direct complement to Tallied. Tallied makes voting information accessible to first-time voters. CCYF Vote16 is working to expand who gets to be a first-time voter - extending voting rights to 16–17 year olds through Bill S-222.

How I contribute
  • Senator outreach
  • Youth petitions
  • Op-eds
04

Milton Public Library

Sept 2025 – Present

Youth Advisory Member

I grew up in libraries. They were my community, my lifeline, the spaces that shaped me into who I am. Now I’m serving them.
What I do
  • Design programming for children
  • Volunteer teaching coding and literacy
  • Strategic input on book acquisitions

Reflection

Most of this work isn’t visible. Most of it doesn’t get an award.

But programming for 2,000 students, one event at a time, is how cultures actually change. I show up because these communities showed up for me first.
